Tiger

The tiger is capable of killing animals over twice its size; it is one of natureÂ´s most feared predators.


  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris
  • Average Length: Male: 8.2 - 13 ft, Female 6.6 - 9 ft.
  • Average Lifespan: 20 - 26 year
  • Habitat: Tropical forests, evergreen forests, woodlands and mangrove swamps to grasslands, savannah and rocky country

Tigers are the largest felines in the world. Many cultures consider the tiger to be a symbol of strength and courage. However, because hunting them is also a sign of bravery in some cultures, tigers are endangered; no more than 3,200 tigers are left in the wild.
